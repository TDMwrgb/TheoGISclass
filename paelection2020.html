PA 2020 Election Results by County

Counties won by Biden, Trump, or Neither

Posted by Theo McGreevey on June 25, 2025

This project visualizes the 2020 U.S. Presidential election results in Pennsylvania, broken down by county. The goal was to identify geographic patterns of political support and determine areas of strong performance for each major party.

The map below color-codes each county based on the winning candidate, with additional consideration for counties with narrow margins.

Data & Methodology

Election results were sourced from the Pennsylvania Department of State. Shapefiles for county boundaries were obtained from the U.S. Census Bureau. Using ArcGIS Pro, counties were joined with voting data and symbolized accordingly.

Findings

Biden won the urban counties such as Philadelphia and Allegheny by significant margins, while Trump performed strongly in most rural counties. Some swing counties like Erie and Northampton played a key role in the final tally.

“Geography is central to understanding American electoral politics.”

This visualization demonstrates the urban-rural divide in modern American elections, especially in swing states like Pennsylvania.
