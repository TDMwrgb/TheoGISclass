Population Density of Northern Miami

Exploring urban patterns through GIS

Posted by Theo McGreevey on June 25, 2025

This map shows population density of a section of miami. This map was made for my seccond mastery assignment, where I learned about two concepts in mapping: e cological falalcy and the Modifiable Unit Area Problem. Ecological Fallacy is the faulty reasoning that individual people/elements will always fit in with a larger trend. For example, you might think everyone in a violent neighborhood is a criminal. In Miami, regions of mostly high-density tracts often had lower density tracts within them. MAUP is the Modifiable Unit Area Problem, which is that changing the grouping or scale of spatial data changes the analysis results of that spatial data. In Miami, many census tracts seemed to have medium population density, but at the block level, most blocks seemed to have low population density. Population density maps by census tract were easier to see on. A larger scale. And since there were less lines it was easier to make different groupings stand out. With Block, I had to zoom in more to make the map understandable. The differences between grouping were not as apparent, and the huge number of lines also made it difficult to interpret the map. I made this map during mt summer online GIS class with professor Bradley Gardener.
